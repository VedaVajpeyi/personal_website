Working premise
Most product problems are social-system problems wearing technical clothes.
01 / Before the fix

The job is to diagnose the misread before the roadmap starts performing certainty.

A field guide like this should not feel like a library dump. It should feel like a better first conversation.

Teams usually say they have an onboarding issue, a motivation issue, or a conversion issue. Sometimes that is true. Often it is the cleanest available story, not the most accurate one. The point of a sociological guide is to slow down that move and force the room to name what kind of problem it is actually looking at.

If the diagnosis changes, the roadmap should too.
02 / The stack

The models are not equal. They are meant to be read as a sequence of better questions.

Behavior
The behavior system

Start here when the room is bouncing between user motivation and interface friction without naming the surrounding culture.

Adoption
The social adoption funnel

Use this when the decisive drop-off happens before the dashboard has a clean event to capture.

Risk
Social risk vs. functional value

Use this when the product solves a real problem but the act still asks the user to expose too much.

Trust
The trust stack

Use this when the interface seems fine but hesitation remains because the weak layer is not functional at all.

Metrics
The metric is a social claim

Use this when the dashboard is moving one way and lived experience is moving another.

Strategy
Value flow

Use this when growth looks elegant because labor, cost, or harm is leaving the frame.

In this version, the guide reads like a hierarchy of questions rather than a wall of modules.

03 / Patterns

The point is not to know more stories. It is to recognize the mechanism underneath them.

Facebook
Context collapse

When too many audiences merge into one stage, expression flattens before users can fully articulate why.

TikTok
Weak ties as architecture

Growth accelerates when discovery moves through bridging structures rather than only through existing follower bonds.

Alexa
The metric named the wrong problem

A number can improve while the behavior it is supposed to represent reverses direction underneath it.

Uber
Ratings as emotional labor

The system does not just measure service quality. It scripts the social performance required to remain viable.

04 / Where this goes

The stronger version of this page would probably be something people cite, teach from, and carry into reviews.

That means the destination should feel less like a product artifact and more like a piece of clear intellectual work. The workshop, the essays, and the PDF can all sit around it. But this version asks whether the center of gravity should be authority and readability first.
